About

I am an investigative reporter based in South Florida. Born in Chicago to Haitian parents, I spent part of my childhood in Haiti before my family eventually settled in Miami, where I won my first writing contest at age 10. I attended Florida State University and Florida International University, where my plans to attend law school took a detour when I began freelancing at The Miami Herald and decided to become a journalist.

After three years at the Herald, I interned at The Chicago Tribune, then worked for the St. Louis Post-Dispatch, where I covered crime. I returned to Florida to work at The Palm Beach Post, where I covered criminal and civil courts in Martin County, then Palm Beach County criminal courts. After more than a decade of courts coverage, in 2018 I loved to the Post's investigation team.

Over the course of my career I have also traveled both domestically and internationally to cover breaking news. This includes several trips to my family homeland of Haiti, where I spent a month covering the aftermath of the 2010 earthquake.

Through the course of my career, I have made several television and radio appearances providing commentary both on cases I've covered and as a journalist with expertise covering legal issues. I am also self-taught as a graphic designer and have completed hundreds of projects, mostly for churches and businesses.
